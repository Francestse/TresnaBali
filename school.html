Our Cooking School

We found our little piece of paradise in 2009 - lush riverside land just outside of Ubud that was pure virgin jungle. We envisioned and created a purpose-built cooking school nestled amoung the towering coconut trees and mature tropical fruit trees such as durian, jackfruit, and rambutan. In this magical place called Tresna Bali Cooking School, avid cooks from all over the world dive deep into Balinese culture and the stories behind the meals.

Beyond our beautiful location, we’re systemic in our approach.

Having organized some of the most exclusive weddings on the island, we apply our event expertise to our passion – authentic Balinese food and traditional living culture. Every element of our kitchen has been designed as live theatre stations where you're one of the main actors!

Pound! Grind! Wrap! Skewer! In not only 1 but 2 kitchens.

Authentic Balinese Home Cooking

Real Balinese food is rather time consuming to prepare but tantalizingly delicious as it’s such a lively, fresh and complex cuisine. Visiting tourists who eat Nasi Goreng or Gado Gado are actually sampling dishes from other parts of Indonesia, but these do not represent real Balinese food. We teach you Adi’s own recipes that he’s honed over decades of cooking for loved ones in his family compound and with the village Banjar. You’ll cook authentic Balinese Ceremonial dishes as well as family home-cooked dishes, not found anywhere else in Bali.

We've crafted 38 tried and true Balinese recipes where you’ll learn 15 of them in one morning!

You'll gather organic fruit, spices, and vegetables from our garden and learn advanced cooking techniques. You can practically taste the goodness in everything you pick! To this bounty, we add sashimi quality seafood from Jimbaran Fish Market, and choice cuts of pork, and free range chickens that we source at 4:30 am each morning at the Ubud local market.

Authentic Balinese Home Cooking

Real Balinese food is rather time consuming to prepare but tantalizingly delicious as it’s such a lively, fresh and complex cuisine. Visiting tourists who eat Nasi Goreng or Gado Gado are actually sampling dishes from other parts of Indonesia, but these do not represent real Balinese food.

Our Menus are Adi’s own recipes that he’s honed over decades of cooking for loved ones in his family compound and with the village Banjar. We teach only authentic Balinese Ceremonial dishes as well as home cooked dishes, not found anywhere else in Bali.

We use the freshest of ingredients such as spices and herbs that you personally pick from our organic gardens! We add sashimi quality seafood from Jimbaran Fish Market and choice cuts of pork, free range chickens and organic vegetables that we source at 4:30 am each morning at the local market in Ubud.

Riverside Location

We selected the location for its natural riverside tranquility away from the tourist beaches. We’ve attended cooking schools around Asia and beyond to learn from the best. We’ve tended our fruit trees for years, planted an organic garden and designed cooking stations that are comfortable and conducive to learning.

Beyond our beautiful location, we’re systemic in our approach. Having organized some of the most exclusive weddings on the island, we are applying our event expertise to our passion – authentic Balinese food and traditional living culture.

About Us

We started as a love story 22 years ago. Frances Tse Ardika, born Canadian and having lived in Hong Kong, London, San Francisco, Tokyo and Toronto as well as having traveled extensively throughout Asia, Europe and the Americas, finally found her paradise and home when she met her Balinese husband, I Made Adi Ardika. Together, they seek the next big food adventure and discover the foods of their beloved island with friends from near and far.
