Why Tresna Bali?

With us, you won’t just see the place we love, you’ll barrel into it – into our traditions, our riverside garden, our kitchens and our Balinese way of life – taking your own special place around our welcoming table.

Tresna means "love" in Balinese which is what we poured into the building of our dream cooking school. It would be a mistake to see us as "just another cooking class". We're not an afterthought at a hotel or a table set up in someone’s family compound.

We’ve turned the concept of a Balinese cooking class on its head, building it from ground up.

Sure, you’ll learn our coveted recipes and fill your stomach to beyond full, but you’ll go deeper: learning about local Balinese culture, pick your own organic ingredients and get to the heart of cooking, experiencing each intriguing element of everything you’ll put in your mouth.

With our shared love of food and cooking, you'll delve into hands-on lessons that feature your hands, not ours.

Authentic Balinese Food

Real Balinese food is a well-hidden secret from tourists.

Why?

Real Balinese Food is elaborate Ceremonial Food.

Or it’s the simplest food cooked behind the walls of a family compound

Visiting tourists who eat Nasi Goreng or Gado Gado are actually sampling dishes from other parts of Indonesia, but these do not represent real Balinese food.

Our Menus are unique.

Adi created original recipes of traditional favourites.

honed over decades of cooking for loved ones in his family compound and with the village Banjar.

We use the freshest of ingredients such as spices and herbs that you personally pick from our organic gardens! We add sashimi quality seafood from Jimbaran Fish Market and choice cuts of pork, free range chickens and organic vegetables that we source at 4:30 am each morning at the local market in Ubud.

You'll learn authentic Balinese Ceremonial dishes as well as family home cooked dishes, not found anywhere else in Bali.
